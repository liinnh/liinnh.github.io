Cocktail Network Explorer

Welcome to the Cocktail Network Explorer! This interactive visualization is for anyone who wants to try a new cocktail! Whether you are an experienced mixologist or just looking to order something new at a bar, this tool should be useful for discovering new drinks similar to ones you like.

How To Use: Each circle or "node" represents a cocktail in the database. Similar cocktails are connected by a line. Click on a node to highlight its connections and double click to view its recipe. Use the dropdown list to find a specific cocktail.

FAQ

Q: Where did you get your data?

A: The cocktails and recipes were pulled from The Cocktail DB, an open source database for cocktail recipes.

Q: How did you decide whether a drink is similar to another drink?

A: All the unique ingredients that appear in the cocktails were grouped into more general categories. For example, the Vodka category includes Vodka, Absolut Vodka, Raspberry Vodka, Vanilla Vodka, and Peach Vodka. Then, the data was one-hot encoded to create dummy variables that indicate whether or not each drink contains each of the general ingredient groups. Then, the cosine similarity between every possible pairing of drinks was computed. If 2 drinks contain similar enough ingredients (similarity greater than a certain threshold value), they are linked to one another.

Q: How come the color or category of some drinks aren't what I would expect?

A: Drinks were categorized based on their primary ingredient, which is usually the first ingredient in the cocktail recipe from the dataset. This isn't always the case, so please reach out if you notice a drink assignment that doesn't make sense and I will reassign it.
